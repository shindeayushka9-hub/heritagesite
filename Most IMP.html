~ Ajanta Caves ~

Ajanta Caves are ancient Buddhist caves located near Aurangabad. They were built around the 2nd century BCE. There are about 30 rock-cut caves carved into the hills. These caves were used by monks for meditation and worship. The walls contain beautiful paintings of Lord Buddha. Many paintings show stories from the Jataka Tales. The caves were rediscovered in 1819. They are famous for their ancient Indian art. Ajanta attracts tourists from all over the world. It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.

~ Ellora Caves ~

Ellora Caves are located near Aurangabad in Maharashtra. They were built between the 6th and 10th centuries. The caves represent Hindu, Buddhist and Jain religions. There are about 34 rock-cut caves. The most famous structure is the Kailasa Temple. It is carved from a single large rock. The carvings show stories from Hindu mythology. The architecture shows great skill of ancient craftsmen. Many tourists visit Ellora every year. It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.

~ Elephanta Caves ~

Elephanta Caves are located on an island near Mumbai. They were built between the 5th and 8th centuries. The caves are mainly dedicated to Lord Shiva. They contain large rock sculptures and carvings. The most famous sculpture is the Trimurti of Shiva. The caves show excellent rock-cut architecture. Tourists reach the island by boat from Gateway of India. The island got its name from a stone elephant statue. The caves are an important historical site. They became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987.

~ Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus ~

CSMT is a historic railway station in Mumbai. It was built in the year 1887. The station was earlier called Victoria Terminus. It is designed in the Victorian Gothic style. The building has beautiful domes and arches. It was designed by architect Frederick William Stevens. CSMT is one of the busiest railway stations in India. Millions of passengers travel through it daily. It is an important landmark of Mumbai. It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2004.

~ Victorian Gothic and Art Deco Buildings ~

This heritage site is located in South Mumbai. The buildings were built during the 19th and 20th centuries. They combine Victorian Gothic and Art Deco architecture. Many buildings are around the famous Oval Maidan. Examples include the High Court and University buildings. Gothic buildings have tall towers and pointed arches. Art Deco buildings have geometric designs. The area has many historic structures. It shows Mumbai’s architectural history. It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2018.

~ Western Ghats ~

The Western Ghats are a long mountain range in India. They stretch for about 1600 km. They pass through several states including Maharashtra. The region is famous for its biodiversity. Many rare plants and animals live here. Several important rivers originate here. The forests help control climate and rainfall. Many national parks are located here. Scientists call it a biodiversity hotspot. It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2012.

~ Kaas Plateau ~

Kaas Plateau is located near Satara in Maharashtra. It is known as the Valley of Flowers of Maharashtra. Thousands of colorful flowers bloom here during monsoon. More than 850 species of plants grow here. Many plants are rare and unique. The best time to visit is August to October. The plateau attracts tourists and photographers. Scientists study the plants here. The area is protected to preserve nature. It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2012.
